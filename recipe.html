Grind the cashews, poppy seeds, and cumin seeds into a smooth paste, using as little water as possible. Set aside. Deep-fry the sliced onions in hot oil, ensuring not to overcrowd. When the onions turn light brown, remove them from the oil and drain on a paper towel. The fried onions will crisp up as they drain. Also, fry the cashew nuts until golden brown. Set aside. Wash the rice and soak it in water for twenty minutes. Meanwhile, in a large wide pan over medium heat, add oil, sliced onions, blended paste, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, and garlic. Fry for a minute. Then add tomatoes and sauté until cooked but not mushy. Add red chili powder, biryani powder, mint, coriander leaves, and sauté well. Mix in yogurt, ensuring to move the skillet away from heat when adding yogurt to prevent curdling. Return the skillet to the stove, add washed lamb, salt, and ½ cup water. Mix well and cook for 1 hour on medium-low heat or pressure cook for 6 whistles. If water is not fully drained, heat it with the lid off. In another large pan, boil thrice the amount of rice you're using. When boiling, add rice, salt, and cumin, and mix well. After exactly 7 minutes, or when the rice is 80% done, switch off and drain the rice. Now, the layering begins. Pat and level the lamb, then add the drained hot rice on top. Garnish with fried onions, ghee, mint, coriander leaves, and saffron dissolved in milk.
